# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Muslims (skewing younger, motivated by self-improvement) who want to grow their
deen as a daily discipline — tracking prayers, Quran, dzikir, sunnah, and sedekah,
and reflecting (muhasabah) on good vs. bad deeds. They open the app in short,
frequent sessions: a quick check of "where am I today / what's next", a fast log,
an occasional deep read. Offline-first, privacy-first (all data on device).

## Product Purpose

Nasuha turns spiritual growth into something you can *feel* progressing — a
training log for the soul. "Level up your soul · level up your amal." Success =
the user comes back daily, logs honestly, and watches a real streak/score climb.
It must motivate without trivializing worship.

## Brand Personality

Energizing · disciplined · devotional. The voice is a committed training partner,
not a soft meditation app and not a gamer overlay. Confident, encouraging,
respectful of the sacred. Think "spiritual gym": effort, progress, accountability —
expressed with restraint, never irreverence.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Material green template** (the current state): seed-based ColorScheme,
  identical tinted cards with thin borders, default Roboto. The #1 thing to escape.
- Soft pastel "kids app" look; toy-like over-rounded shapes.
- Loud neon/RGB "gamer" aesthetic that breaks the devotional tone.
- Cluttered identical card grids with no hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **Progress you can feel.** Score, streak and level read like a training HUD —
   the home answers "where am I today, what's next" at a glance.
2. **Strength meets stillness.** Energetic and motivating, but reverent; it's
   worship, not a game. Calm surfaces, decisive accents.
3. **Earn it, don't decorate it.** Gold, glow and intensity are reserved for real
   achievement (streaks, levels, completion) — never default chrome.
4. **Identity over template.** A committed dark-first palette and a distinct
   typeface; never mistakable for stock Material.
5. **Honest by design.** Muhasabah counts the bad as well as the good; the UI
   shows both without shaming — accountability, not guilt.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Dark-first, with a maintained light theme (system option honored).
- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast; large/number displays ≥ 3:1. No light-gray-on-tint.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (streak/level animations get a static fallback).
- Indonesian-language UI; Arabic rendered RTL with a proper Arabic face.
- Tap targets ≥ 44px; never rely on color alone (positive/negative also use icon + sign).
